# The Incremental State

### The Philosophy Behind "The Incremental State"

In tech, Data Engineering and Software Engineering are often treated as two entirely separate disciplines. The truth is simpler: **processing data, especially at scale and in distributed systems, is a software engineering problem.** 

You cannot build reliable data products without automated testing, robust system design, and a stategic architecture. As soon as you realize that, the boundary between the two disciplines disappears.

---

### Why the blog name "The Incremental State"?


I chose this blog name because each word represents a core concept in its respective field.

* **Incremental:** In Data Engineering, recomputing the entire history, every time when a new record arrives, is rarely what you want, not if you care about your performance and your budget.

* **State**: Without state, a system has no memory. E.g., in both streaming an batch processing, it's the only anchor that tells the system what to do next.

On a more philosophical level: The *Incremental State* reflects how I approach engineering. You cannot improve a system or your own skills by standing still. Growth only happens when the state changes.
